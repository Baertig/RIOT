    1 # Reporting Guidelines {#coc-reporting-guide}
    2 
    3 If you believe someone is violating the [code of conduct][CoC] we ask that you
    4 report it to us by emailing <conduct@riot-os.org>. Currently, the recipients of
    5 this email address are [\@OlegHahm](https://github.com/OlegHahm) (Oleg Hahm) and
    6 [\@miri64](https://github.com/miri64) (Martine Lenders).
    7 
    8 **All reports will be kept confidential.** In some cases we may determine that a
    9 public statement will need to be made. If that's the case, the identities of all
    10 victims and reporters will remain confidential unless those individuals instruct
    11 us otherwise.
    12 
    13 **If you believe anyone is in physical danger, please notify appropriate law
    14 enforcement first.** If you are unsure what law enforcement agency is
    15 appropriate, please include this in your report and we will attempt to notify
    16 them.
    17 
    18 If you are unsure whether the incident is a violation, or whether the space
    19 where it happened is covered by this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to still
    20 report it. We would much rather have a few extra reports where we decide to take
    21 no action, rather than miss a report of an actual violation. We do not look
    22 negatively on you if we find the incident is not a violation. And knowing about
    23 incidents that are not violations, or happen outside our spaces, can also help
    24 us to improve the Code of Conduct or the processes surrounding it.
    25 
    26 In your report please include:
    27 
    28 - Your contact info (so we can get in touch with you if we need to follow up)
    29 - Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved. If there
    30  were other witnesses besides you, please try to include them as well.
    31 - When and where the incident occurred. Please be as specific as possible.
    32 - Your account of what occurred. If there is a publicly available record (e.g.
    33  forum post, a mailing list archive, or a public Matrix chat link) please include a link.
    34 - Any extra context you believe existed for the incident.
    35 - If you believe this incident is ongoing.
    36 - Any other information you believe we should have.
    37 
    38 ### What happens after you file a report?
    39 
    40 You will receive an email from one of the core community members as soon as
    41 possible. We promise to acknowledge receipt within 24 hours (and will aim for
    42 much quicker than that).
    43 
    44 They will review the incident and determine:
    45 
    46 - What happened.
    47 - Whether this event constitutes a code of conduct violation.
    48 - Who the bad actor was.
    49 - Whether this is an ongoing situation, or if there is a threat to anyone's
    50  physical safety.
    51 
    52 If this is determined to be an ongoing incident or a threat to physical safety,
    53 their immediate priority will be to protect everyone involved.
    54 This means we may delay an "official" response until we believe that the
    55 situation has ended and that everyone is physically safe.
    56 
    57 Once the working group has a complete account of the events they will make a
    58 decision as to how to response. Responses may include:
    59 
    60 - Nothing (if we determine no violation occurred).
    61 - A private reprimand from us to the individual(s) involved.
    62 - A public reprimand.
    63 - An imposed vacation (i.e. asking someone to "take a week off" from the forum, the Matrix chats, or a mailing
    64  list).
    65 - A permanent or temporary ban from some or all RIOT spaces (forum, Matrix chats, mailing lists,
    66  etc.)
    67 - A request for a public or private apology.
    68 
    69 We'll respond within one week to the person who filed the report with either a
    70 resolution or an explanation of why the situation is not yet resolved.
    71 
    72 Once we've determined our final action, we'll contact the original reporter to
    73 let them know what action (if any) we'll be taking. We'll take into account
    74 feedback from the reporter on the appropriateness of our response, but we don't
    75 guarantee we'll act on it.
