    1 RIOT-OS Code of Conduct {#coc}
    2 =======================
    3 
    4 RIOT, like so many other free and open source software projects, is made up of a
    5 mixed group of professionals and volunteers from all over the world, working on
    6 every aspect of our goals - including teaching, steering, and connecting people.
    7 
    8 We want diversity to be one of our greatest strengths, but it can also lead to
    9 communication issues and unhappiness. This is why we ask people to adhere to a
    10 few ground rules. They apply equally to founders, maintainers, contributors and
    11 those seeking help and guidance.
    12 
    13 This is not meant to be an exhaustive list of things you are not allowed to do.
    14 We rather would like you to think of it as a guide to enrich our community and
    15 the technical community in general with new knowledge and perspectives by
    16 allowing everyone to participate.
    17 
    18 This code of conduct applies to all spaces managed by the RIOT community. This
    19 includes any communication within the RIOT community, both online and face to
    20 face. In addition, violations of this code outside these spaces may also
    21 affect a person's ability to participate within them.
    22 
    23 If you believe someone is violating the code of conduct, we ask that you report
    24 it by emailing <conduct@riot-os.org>. For more details please see our [Reporting
    25 Guidelines](https://doc.riot-os.org/coc-reporting-guide.html).
    26 
    27 - **Be friendly and patient.**
    28 - **Be welcoming.** We strive to be a community that welcomes and supports
    29  people of all backgrounds and identities. This includes, but is not limited
    30  to members of any race, ethnicity, culture, national origin, colour,
    31  immigration status, social and economic class, educational level, sex,
    32  sexual orientation, gender identity and expression, age, size, family
    33  status, political belief, religion, and mental and physical ability.
    34 - **Be considerate.** Your work will be used by other people, and you in turn
    35  will depend on the work of others. Any decision you take will affect users
    36  and colleagues, and you should take those consequences into account when
    37  making decisions. Remember that we're a world-wide community, so you might
    38  not be communicating in someone else's primary language.
    39 - **Be respectful.** Not all of us will agree all the time, but disagreement
    40  is no excuse for poor behavior and poor manners. We might all experience
    41  some frustration now and then, but we cannot allow that frustration to turn
    42  into a personal attack. It’s important to remember that a community where
    43  people feel uncomfortable or threatened is not a productive one. Members of
    44  the RIOT community should be respectful when dealing with other members as
    45  well as with people outside the RIOT community.
    46 - **Be careful in the words that you choose.** We are a community of
    47  professionals, and we conduct ourselves professionally. Be kind to others.
    48  Do not insult or put down other participants. Harassment and other
    49  exclusionary behavior aren't acceptable. This includes, but is not limited
    50  to:
    51  - Violent threats or language directed against another person.
    52  - Discriminatory jokes and language.
    53  - Posting sexually explicit or violent material.
    54  - Posting (or threatening to post) other people's personally identifying
    55  information ("doxing").
    56  - Personal insults, especially those using racist or sexist terms.
    57  - Unwelcome sexual attention.
    58  - Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behavior.
    59  - Repeated harassment of others. In general, if someone asks you to stop,
    60  then stop.
    61 - **When we disagree, try to understand why.** Disagreements, both social and
    62  technical, happen all the time and RIOT is no exception. It is important
    63  that we resolve disagreements and differing views constructively. Remember
    64  that we’re different. The strength of RIOT comes from its varied community,
    65  people from a wide range of backgrounds. Different people have different
    66  perspectives on issues. Being unable to understand why someone holds a
    67  viewpoint doesn’t mean that they’re wrong. Don’t forget that it is human to
    68  err and blaming each other doesn’t get us anywhere. Instead, focus on
    69  helping to resolve issues and learning from mistakes.
    70 
    71 Text based on the Code of Conduct of the [Django
    72 community](https://www.djangoproject.com/conduct/).
